How To Choose A High Quality Domain For Your PBN

Selecting the right expired domain is the foundation of a successful PBN. Start by examining the domain's backlink profile - look for natural, high-authority links from relevant sources. A diverse link profile with a mix of dofollow and nofollow links appears more natural to search engines.

Check the domain's history using tools like the Wayback Machine. Avoid domains with spammy pasts or that were used for questionable purposes. A clean history with quality content signals trust to search engines.

Evaluate the domain's metrics, but don't rely on them exclusively. While DA/PA scores provide a quick reference, dive deeper into the referring domains' quality. A domain with fewer high-quality links often outperforms one with many low-quality ones.

Consider the domain's relevance to your niche. A thematically related domain passes more SEO value to your money site. The closer the domain's previous content matches your target niche, the more powerful the link juice will be.

Finally, check for any existing penalties or blacklists. A clean domain with steady traffic history and no manual actions will serve as a strong asset in your PBN network for years to come.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about PBN domains and our services

What is a PBN domain?

+

A PBN (Private Blog Network) domain is a previously owned domain that has been repurposed to build backlinks to your money site. These domains typically have existing backlinks, domain authority, and trust factors that can help boost your SEO efforts.

How do I know if a domain is good for PBN?

+

Look for domains with:

  • Clean backlink profile (no spammy or toxic links)
  • Good domain authority (DA) and page authority (PA) scores
  • Relevant niche history
  • No Google penalties
  • Natural link profile with diverse referring domains
  • Clean WHOIS history

How do I set up a PBN with these domains?

+

Setting up a PBN involves several steps:

  1. Register the domain with private WHOIS information
  2. Set up separate hosting for each domain
  3. Install WordPress or another CMS
  4. Create unique, high-quality content
  5. Add your money site links naturally within the content
  6. Maintain the sites with regular updates and security patches

How many domains do I need for a PBN?

+

The number of domains you need depends on your SEO goals and budget. A small PBN might have 5-10 domains, while larger networks can have 50+ domains. Focus on quality over quantity - a few high-authority domains are more valuable than many low-quality ones. Start small and scale as you see results.

What's the difference between expired and auction domains?

+

Expired domains have been dropped by their previous owners and are available for registration by anyone. Auction domains are being sold by their current owners, often at a premium price. Expired domains can be more affordable but require more vetting, while auction domains are typically pre-vetted but more expensive.

How do you vet your domains?

+

We use a rigorous vetting process that includes:

  • Checking for manual actions or penalties in Google Search Console
  • Analyzing backlink profiles for spam or toxic links
  • Verifying domain history and previous content
  • Checking for blacklisting or spam flags
  • Ensuring clean WHOIS history
  • Verifying metrics like DA, PA, and referring domains
